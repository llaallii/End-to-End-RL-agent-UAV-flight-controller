Test document for development of Doorstop

1.1 SAF001

The Safety Monitor task shall execute at 100 Hz as the highest-priority RTOS task.

Parent links: SYS001

1.1 SAF010

A geofence of +/-500 m radius from the home point shall be enforced; breach shall trigger safety state escalation.

1.2 SAF002

A hardware watchdog timer with a 50 ms timeout shall be implemented; failure to reset shall trigger the EMERGENCY state.

1.2 SAF020

Emergency landing shall execute a vertical descent at 1 m/s with touchdown detection (accelerometer) and automatic disarm on ground contact.

1.3 SAF003

IMU data staleness shall be detected if no valid reading is received within 1 ms; the fault shall be reported to the Safety Monitor.

1.4 SAF004

Barometer data timeout shall be detected at >20 ms; GPS no-fix timeout shall be detected at >30 s.

1.5 SAF005

GPS quality shall be flagged as degraded when HDOP >5.0 or satellite count <4.

1.6 SAF006

Magnetometer data timeout shall be detected at >15 ms, and magnetic interference shall be flagged.

1.7 SAF007

The system shall enforce a hard altitude limit of 100 m AGL.

1.8 SAF008

The system shall enforce a maximum tilt angle of +/-45 degrees (roll and pitch).

1.9 SAF009

The system shall enforce a maximum horizontal velocity of 15 m/s and a maximum vertical velocity of 5 m/s.

1.11 SAF011

An RL inference watchdog with a 5 ms timeout shall monitor each inference cycle; timeout shall trigger automatic fallback to the PID controller.

1.12 SAF012

A PID fallback controller shall be loaded and ready for immediate activation at all times during RL-mode flight.

1.13 SAF013

Motor mixer saturation exceeding 90% shall generate a WARNING-level alert.

1.14 SAF014

ESC heartbeat loss shall be detected and reported to the Safety Monitor.

1.15 SAF015

Battery voltage shall be monitored at 10 Hz; voltage <3.5 V/cell shall trigger RTL, <3.3 V/cell shall trigger emergency landing, and <3.0 V/cell shall trigger emergency shutdown.

1.16 SAF016

Total motor current shall be limited to a maximum of 60 A.

1.17 SAF017

Board temperature shall be monitored at 1 Hz; temperature >70C shall trigger power reduction, and >85C shall trigger emergency shutdown.

1.18 SAF018

The flight safety system shall implement a 5-state FSM: NORMAL, WARNING, CRITICAL, EMERGENCY, SHUTDOWN.

1.19 SAF019

Transition from EMERGENCY to SHUTDOWN (motor kill) shall complete in <1 ms.

1.21 SAF021

The following events shall trigger immediate EMERGENCY state: manual kill switch, attitude error >45 degrees, IMU failure, battery <3.0 V/cell, thermal >85C.

1.22 SAF022

RC signal loss >5 s shall escalate to CRITICAL state; telemetry loss >30 s shall escalate to WARNING state.

1.23 SAF023

RL inference timeout >10 ms shall trigger a WARNING and automatic switch to the PID fallback controller.

1.24 SAF024

The system shall block arming if sensor calibration is invalid, GPS fix is not acquired (in outdoor mode), or battery is <3.7 V/cell.

1.25 SAF025

If RL neural network weights are not loaded, the system shall warn the operator and allow arming in PID-only mode.

1.26 SAF026

A black-box flight data logger shall record sensor data, control signals, fault events, and battery telemetry to SD card at 100 Hz.

1.27 SAF027

Crash detection shall trigger on accelerometer readings >4 g or attitude exceeding +/-90 degrees (flip detection), followed by immediate disarm and log dump.

1.28 SAF028

Fault codes shall be indicated via LED blink patterns (1-9 blinks) and buzzer audio alerts.
