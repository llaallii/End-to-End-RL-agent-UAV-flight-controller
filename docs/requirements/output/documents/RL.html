Test document for development of Doorstop

1.1 RL001

The RL observation space shall include at minimum: position (3), velocity (3), attitude (3), angular rates (3), and goal position (3), totaling >=15 dimensions.

Parent links: SYS001

1.1 RL010

The RL framework selection process shall evaluate >=3 candidate frameworks with a documented trade study.

1.2 RL002

All observation inputs shall be normalized to approximately [-1, 1] or standard-normal range using collected training statistics.

1.3 RL003

The action space shall output 4 motor commands bounded in the range [-1, 1] via Tanh activation.

1.4 RL004

The reward function shall encourage goal-seeking behavior and penalize crashes, with tunable weighting parameters.

1.5 RL005

Episode termination shall trigger on crash, success condition, or a maximum-time timeout.

1.6 RL006

Domain randomization shall vary at minimum: mass, inertia, motor gains, and sensor noise levels during training.

1.7 RL007

The actor neural network shall be a feed-forward MLP with 2 hidden layers of 128 neurons each using ReLU activation.

1.8 RL008

The deployed actor network shall contain <=19,076 parameters (~19 K INT8 or ~76 KB FP32).

1.9 RL009

The critic (value-function) head shall be used during training only and shall NOT be deployed on the MCU.

1.11 RL011

The RL training pipeline shall execute >=3 training iterations with performance analysis between each.

1.12 RL012

The trained RL policy shall achieve stable hover for >=60 seconds in simulation.

1.13 RL013

The RL policy shall meet or exceed the PID baseline on at least tracking error, settling time, and control effort metrics.

1.14 RL014

The RL policy evaluation shall test generalization across unseen mass variations, wind disturbances, and sensor noise levels.

1.15 RL015

Final convergence validation shall confirm that the training reward curve has plateaued and performance is stable across >=3 random seeds.

1.16 RL016

The compressed RL policy shall occupy <1 MB of storage, targeting ~24 KB (INT8 weights + activation buffers).

1.17 RL017

Post-quantization (INT8) performance degradation shall be <5% relative to FP32 on all key metrics.

1.18 RL018

The compressed policy shall be exported in an embedded-friendly format (ONNX, TFLite, or custom C array).
